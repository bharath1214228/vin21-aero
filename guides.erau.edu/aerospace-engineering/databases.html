The following are recommended databases for locating scholarly articles, dissertations, theses, and conference papers on aerospace engineering. 

  • Advanced Technologies & Aerospace Database This link opens in a new window
    Provides citations, abstracts and some full text articles to aeronautics, astronautics, computer & information technology, meteorology, communications and space sciences journal articles, conference proceedings, NASA documents, and technical reports.
    Vendor: ProQuest
    Coverage: 1962 - present
    Full Text: Partial

    Search Tips
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of the root of a word to find all variants: automat* will find automate, automated, automatic, and automation.
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: "crew resource management."
    • To locate a complete issue of a journal, click on Publications at the top of the screen.
    • Use connectors AND, OR, NOT to separate terms and phrases: "flight crew" AND fatigue.

    Requesting Items
    • If full text is not available, mark the records you want by checking the box in the upper left corner of the record.
    • When you have finished marking all the items you want, click the Email link near the top of the results page.
    • Select Citation, abstract, indexing from the drop-down menu next to Content.
    • Enter your own email address and click on Continue.
    • After you receive the email containing your records, forward the email to library@erau.edu. Please include:
      • Your name
      • Daytime phone number
      • Course number
      • Whether you’re an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD student
      • Whether you’re a Daytona Beach or Worldwide student
  • AIAA Aerospace Research Central (ARC) This link opens in a new window
    Journals, standards, ebooks and papers published by the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA).
    Check the library catalog for standards owned by the Hunt Library.

    Vendor: American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics (AIAA)
    Coverage: Journals: Varies by publication | Papers: 1963 - present
    Full Text: Partial

    Search Tips
    • Enter search terms in the Search box located at the top right.
    • When two or more words are entered, the database will automatically place AND between the words.
    • The operators OR and NOT can be used but must be uppercase.
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search them as an exact phrase, such as a book title: "From Rainbow to Gusto."
    • Use the asterisk (*) to find variant endings of a root word: plan* will find plan, plans, planning, etc. The asterisk cannot be used within quotation marks for phrase searching.
    • To find a specific AIAA paper, change the drop-down menu to the search box from Anywhere to Find by Paper Number
    • To browse journals, books, or standards, click on the respective tab located along the top.

    Requesting Items
    If full text is not available, copy and paste the item information into an email and send it to library@erau.edu. Please include:
    • Your name
    • Daytime phone number
    • Course number
    • Whether you’re an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD student
    • Whether you’re a Daytona Beach or Worldwide student
  • IEEE Xplore Digital Library This link opens in a new window
    Journals, magazines, conference proceedings, transactions and standards in areas such as electrical engineering, computer science, electronics, media and more.
    Vendor: Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)
    Coverage: 1872 - present
    Full Text: Partial

    Video Tutorials

    Good to Know
    Click Create Account in the upper right corner of the database page to access additional features such as Alerts, Search History, etc.

    Search Tips
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: “aircraft instrumentation”
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of the root of a word to find all variants: optic* will find optics, optical, etc.
    • Click Advanced Search to access additional options such as specific field searching, Boolean (AND/OR/NOT) searching, and Publication Date ranges.
    • Once results are obtained, click on PDF below the citation to view the complete document.
    • For help using and searching this database, click Help or Get Help in the menu bar across the top of the screen on any database page.

    Requesting Items
    If full text is not available, copy the records you want, paste them into an email, and send them to library@erau.edu. Please include:

    • Daytime phone number
    • Course number
    • Whether you’re an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD student

  • SAE MOBILUS This link opens in a new window
    Technical papers, standards, ebooks, and related publications from SAE International.
    Vendor: Society of Automotive Engineers
    Coverage: 1906 - present
    Full Text: Partial

    Video Tutorials

    Search Tips
    • Click on Search in the top ribbon next to SAE Mobilus Home
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of the root of a word to find all variants: brak* will find brake, brakes, and braking.
    • To look for a term in a specific field such as Abstract, Title, etc., use the in: drop-down menu located next to the search box.
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: "brake fluid".
    • Use AND between search terms and phrases to search for mutliple terms: "braking system" AND aircraft. Or, click on the Advanced Search link to type separate words and phrases in individual boxes.
    • To access the full-text of an item, click on the title and then the Download button.
    • For help using and searching this database, click on the Help? button located at the top right. This gives you access to the User Guide, How to videos, and other help tools.

    Requesting Items
    • If full text is not available, mark the records you want by checking the box next to the record.
    • When you have finished marking all the items you want, click the Share link at the bottom of the page.
    • Enter your own email address in the Recipients section and click on Submit to Send Email.
    • After you receive the email containing your records, forward the email to library@erau.edu. Please include:
      • Your name
      • Daytime phone number
      • Course number
      • Whether you’re an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD student
      • Whether you’re a Daytona Beach or Worldwide student.

  • Web of Science This link opens in a new window
    Provides access to citation/abstract details for hundreds of thousands of worldwide publications such as journals (including open access journals), conference papers, books, and book chapters. Includes journal impact factors in the Journal Citation Reports tab.
    Vendor: Clarivate Analytics
    Coverage: 1980 - present
    Full Text: None

    Training Portal (video, guides, etc.)

    Good to Know
    • To create a free Web of Science account, click the Register icon in the upper right corner of the page.
    • To use advanced features such as saved searches, alerts, etc., sign in with a Web of Science username and password.
    • Click the Question Mark icon in the lower right corner of any page to access a variety of help options.
    • Web of Science includes a subscription to Journal Citation Reports (JCR), a tool which analyzes citation references, measures research influence and impact at the journal and category levels, and shows the relationship between citing and cited journals. To learn more about the JCR, please see the following sources:
      • Journal Citation Reports Quick Reference Guide
      • Journal Citation Reports: Learn the Basics

    Search Tips
    • To search the full range of dates back to 1980, change the Search in: field to All Databases.
    • To perform a simple keyword search on a topic, enter the desired term(s) in the text box in the center of the page. Change the field to search in or add rows or date ranges as desired.
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of the root of a word to find all variants: climat* will find climate, climatic, climatology, etc.
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: "climate change"
    • After results are retrieved, use the Refine Results options on the left side of the page to get more focused, relevant information.
    • Click the AUTHORS or CITED REFERENCES tabs or the Advanced Search link for more search options.

Browse Databases

The following are recommended databases for locating technical reports (technical and scientific research) on aerospace engineering.  

  • NACA Archive
    Contains a comprehensive collection of full-text NACA publications. The National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA, 1915-1958) was the predecessor of NASA. Coverage: 1917-1958
  • DTIC Online: Public Technical Reports This link opens in a new window
    Searches declassified Department of Defense and government-funded scientific, technical, engineering, and business-related documents from the Defense Technical Information Center.
    Vendor: Defense Technical Information Center
    Coverage: Varies
    Full Text: Partial

    Good to Know
    • Documents are sometimes reclassified and no longer accessible.
    • Click on a document title to view the citation/abstract page. If the item is available full-text, a link titled View the full text of this report will be present.
    • Some documents are hundreds of pages. Please check the pagination (found on the citation/abstract page) before downloading to ensure sure your Internet connection can accomodate it.
    • DTIC does not support wildcard (truncation) searching.

    Search Tips
    • For a general topic search, enter desired search terms in the box labeled Search this site, DoD S&T Reports, and more... in the upper center of the page: aircraft pilot fatigue
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: “joint strike fighter”

    Requesting Items
    If full text is not available, copy and paste the records you want into an email and send them to library@erau.edu. Please include:
    • Your name
    • Daytime phone number
    • Course number
    • Whether you’re an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD student
    • Whether you’re a Daytona Beach or Worldwide student
  • NASA Technical Reports Server (NTRS) This link opens in a new window

eBook Databases
  • AccessEngineering This link opens in a new window
    McGraw-Hill resources and publications including instructional videos and classic books such as Marks’ Standard Handbook for Mechanical Engineers and Perry’s Chemical Engineers Handbook.
    Vendor: McGraw-Hill
    Full Text: Complete

    Good to Know
    • To create a free AccessEngineering account, click on the My account button on the right side of the AccessEngineering homepage. Then, choose Log in via email/username and click on Register.
    • To use features including printing, document downloading, and annotating text, sign in with an AccessEngineering username and password.
    • Downloadable Excel Spreadsheet Calculators covering a range of common calculations across several disciplines are available. Click on Spreadsheets.
    • If you have access issues, clear your browser's cache and cookies. If you are using the Chrome browser, please try an alternate browser such as Firefox.

    Search Tips
    • To conduct a search with multiple terms or phrases, use connectors AND, OR, NOT to separate terms and phrases: paper AND core
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of the root of a word to find all variants: automat* will find automate, automated, automatic, and automation.
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: "paper core”
    • To view other sections available in a book, click on the book title (Source) below the citation.
    • To view resources in a particular subject area, click on the Subject button, then the desired subject, and then Browse Selected.
    • Pop-up blockers may interfere, so please allow pop-ups.
    • For help in searching this database, click on the Help link at the top of the page.
  • ASM Handbooks Online This link opens in a new window
    Information on ferrous and non-ferrous metals and materials technology.
    Vendor: American Society for Metals
    Full Text: Partial

    Good to Know
    • The database searches across all ASM handbooks some of which may not be available in full text.
    • A green check enclosed in a circle icon quickly alerts you of full text access; a shopping cart icon means no access.
    • On a search results page, under Update Search, scroll to the very bottom of the menu to limit or narrow your results to content that is full text by selecting the Available, Open Access or Free boxes.
    • All ASM handbooks and the ASM Desk Editions are available in full text.

    Search Tips
    • Search by material name, tradename, or designation by using the search box in the top center of the screen.
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: “copper alloys”
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of the root of a word to find all variants: cast* will find cast, casting, castability, etc.
    • Connect search terms with AND: Aluminum AND alloys

    Requesting Items
    If full text is not available, copy the records you want, paste them into an email, and send them to library@erau.edu. Please include:
    • Your name
    • Daytime phone number
    • Course number
    • Whether you’re an undergraduate, graduate, or PhD student
    • Whether you’re a Daytona Beach or Worldwide student
  • Ebook Central This link opens in a new window
    Extensive collection of ebooks on a variety of subjects, such as aviation and human factors.
    Vendor: ProQuest
    Full Text: Partial
    Help:http://proquest.libguides.com/ebookcentral

    Help Guide
    Video Tutorials

    Good to Know
    • Bookshelves are based on your ERNIE login. (Older versions of bookshelves were overridden.)
    • A separate Ebook Central account is no longer required for the use of Bookshelf, highlighting, and downloading features.
    • You can add books to your Bookshelf by clicking on the Add to Bookshelf link located on the left of the book’s Detail Page or by placing a bookmark on any page within the book.
    • For most books the print/download limit is 40% of the book's pages. Printing or downloading a chapter or page range do the same thing and deduct in the same way based on the pages allowed. Both options create a PDF that does not expire.
    • Check the "Availability" section, on the Detail Page, or in the Book Details side panel to see how many copies are available and if it is for Online reading or Download, or both.
    • You can change the language of the interface by clicking on Settings in the naviagation bar and clicking on Language. Choose your language and save the changes.

    Search Tips
    • Enclose terms in quotation marks to search for exact phrases: "crew resource management."
    • Use connectors AND, OR, NOT to separate terms and phrases: "flight crew" AND fatigue. Connectors must be capitalized.
    • Use an asterisk (*) at the end of a word root to find all variants: automat* will find automate, automated, automatic, and automation.
    • Click the Read Online button to view the full text of a book. Or, click on Full Download to download, if available.
    • Use the table of contents or navigational arrows to browse.
    • Use the "Search within book" search box to search for a term or phrase within the book.
    • For help using and searching in the database, click the question mark icon (?) in the upper right corner, or use their Help Guide

    Request Items
    • Books not available full text online in this database may be available in print. Search for the title in EAGLEsearch to see if the library owns a print copy. Visit the Borrow and Renew (Access Services - Circulation) page for help in checking out material.
    • If you see that a book is "Available on request," you may read the book online for up to 5 minutes; click Read Online to do so.
    • Click on the Request button or request this book link to fill out the form to request the library purchase it if you cannot locate it in our catalog.
